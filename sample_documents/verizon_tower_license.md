## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Licensor owns and operates a wireless communications tower facility located at [Property Address] (the "Tower Site"), pursuant to that certain Ground Lease Agreement dated February 22, 2026, as amended by that certain First Amendment to Ground Lease Agreement dated March 15, 2028, between Licensor and the underlying landowner;

WHEREAS, Licensee desires to install, operate, and maintain wireless communications equipment on the Tower Site;

WHEREAS, Licensor has the right to sublicense space on the Tower Site pursuant to the Ground Lease Agreement and First Amendment;

NOW, THEREFORE, the parties agree as follows:

---

## ARTICLE 1: LICENSED SPACE

**1.1 Tower Space.** Licensor hereby grants to Licensee a non-exclusive license to install, operate, and maintain wireless communications equipment on the Tower at the following locations:
- **Antenna Space:** Approximately 142 feet RAD center on the tower structure, consisting of space for up to six (6) panel antennas and three (3) remote radio units (RRUs)
- **Ground Space:** Approximately 100 square feet at the base of the tower for one (1) equipment cabinet (dimensions not to exceed 30" × 32" × 72") and associated equipment pad

**1.2 Equipment Manifest.** Licensee's initial equipment installation shall consist of the following (the "Permitted Equipment"):

| Item | Quantity | Description | Location |
|------|----------|-------------|----------|
| Panel Antenna | 6 | CommScope FFHH-65C-R3-V2, tri-band | 142ft RAD, sectors α/β/γ |
| Remote Radio Unit | 3 | Nokia AirScale AAHF, Band 2/4/66 | 142ft, mounted behind antennas |
| Equipment Cabinet | 1 | Purcell PPC-3E outdoor cabinet | Ground level, concrete pad |
| DC Power Plant | 1 | Eltek Flatpack2, 48V, 12kW | Inside equipment cabinet |
| Battery Backup | 1 | EnerSys PowerSafe, 8-hour reserve | Inside equipment cabinet |
| Coaxial Cable | 6 runs | Andrew LDF7-50A, 1-5/8" | Tower face to cabinet |
| Fiber Trunk | 1 | 12-strand single-mode OS2 | Tower base to cabinet |
| GPS Antenna | 1 | Trimble Bullet III | Tower top |

**1.3 Modifications.** Licensee shall not modify, add to, or replace any Permitted Equipment without prior written notice to Licensor and approval by Licensor's structural engineer, which approval shall not be unreasonably withheld.

---

## ARTICLE 2: TERM AND RENEWAL

**2.1 Initial Term.** The initial term of this License Agreement shall be **five (5) years**, commencing on the Commencement Date and ending on March 14, 2033 (the "Initial Term").

**2.2 Renewal Terms.** This License Agreement shall automatically renew for **four (4) successive renewal terms of five (5) years each** (each a "Renewal Term"), unless Licensee provides written notice of non-renewal at least one hundred eighty (180) days prior to the expiration of the then-current term. The total potential term is **twenty-five (25) years**.

---

## ARTICLE 3: LICENSE FEES

**3.1 Monthly License Fee.** Licensee shall pay to Licensor a monthly license fee ("License Fee") of **Four Thousand Eight Hundred Dollars ($4,800.00)**, payable in advance on the first day of each calendar month.

**3.2 Annual Escalation.** The License Fee shall increase by **three percent (3%)** on each annual anniversary of the Commencement Date.

**3.3 Fee Breakdown.** For reference, the License Fee is calculated as follows:
- Tower antenna space (142ft RAD): $2,800.00/month
- Ground equipment space (100 sq ft): $1,200.00/month
- Power allocation (12kW @ $0.12/kWh): $800.00/month (estimated, actual billed quarterly)

**3.4 Utilities.** Licensee shall reimburse Licensor for actual electrical power consumed by Licensee's equipment, measured by a dedicated submeter installed at Licensor's expense. Power reimbursement shall be billed quarterly at the prevailing utility rate plus a ten percent (10%) administrative fee.

---

## ARTICLE 4: INSTALLATION AND MAINTENANCE

**4.1 Installation Standards.** All equipment installation shall comply with:
- TIA-222-H structural loading standards
- OSHA 1926.502 fall protection requirements
- FCC RF emissions limits (47 CFR Part 1.1310)
- Local building code and zoning requirements
- Licensor's standard installation specifications (Exhibit C)

**4.2 Structural Loading.** Licensee warrants that its Permitted Equipment, as specified in Section 1.2, has been included in a structural analysis of the Tower performed by a licensed professional engineer, and that the Tower can safely support the equipment loading. Licensee's estimated equipment loading:
- Antenna loading: 340 lbs (wind + dead load)
- Cable loading: 180 lbs
- Ground equipment: 2,400 lbs (not tower-structural)

**4.3 Maintenance Access.** Licensee shall have 24/7 access to the Tower Site for maintenance and emergency purposes, subject to Licensor's standard site access procedures and RF safety protocols.

**4.4 Maintenance Obligations.** Licensee shall maintain its equipment in good working order and shall:
- Conduct quarterly visual inspections
- Perform annual RF emissions compliance verification
- Repair or replace damaged equipment within thirty (30) days of discovery
- Maintain proper labeling on all equipment and cabling

---

## ARTICLE 5: INSURANCE AND INDEMNIFICATION

**5.1 Required Insurance.** Licensee shall maintain:
- Commercial General Liability: $5,000,000 per occurrence / $10,000,000 aggregate
- Property Insurance: Full replacement value of Licensee's equipment
- Workers' Compensation: As required by applicable law
- Automobile Liability: $1,000,000 combined single limit

Licensor shall be named as additional insured on the CGL policy.

**5.2 Indemnification.** Licensee shall indemnify and hold harmless Licensor from claims arising from Licensee's equipment, operations, or personnel on the Tower Site.

---

## ARTICLE 6: INTERFERENCE

**6.1 Non-Interference.** Licensee's equipment shall not cause harmful interference to any equipment installed on the Tower prior to Licensee's installation. In the event of interference, Licensee shall, at its sole cost, take immediate steps to eliminate such interference, including powering down equipment if necessary.

**6.2 Protection from Interference.** Licensor shall not permit subsequent licensees to install equipment that causes harmful interference to Licensee's equipment. Priority of interference protection shall be based on installation date.

---

## ARTICLE 7: TERMINATION AND REMOVAL

**7.1 Termination by Licensee.** Licensee may terminate this License Agreement at any time upon one hundred eighty (180) days' prior written notice.

**7.2 Equipment Removal.** Upon termination, Licensee shall remove all Permitted Equipment within one hundred eighty (180) days, at Licensee's sole cost and expense.

**7.3 Default.** Failure to pay License Fees within thirty (30) days after written notice of non-payment shall constitute an event of default. In the event of default, Licensor may terminate this License Agreement upon sixty (60) days' additional written notice if such default remains uncured.
